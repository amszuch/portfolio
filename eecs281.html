EECS 498: XR

Extended Reality and Society

Project 1: CSE Simulator

For this project, I worked with one other partner. We were tasked with creating a "Job Simulator"-type VR environment mirroring a computer lab at the BBB on North Campus. Key elements to include were lights, fire alarms, whiteboards, printers, and NPCs with various specs for interaction. Additionally, we needed to design our own custom object and interaction. The two we chose to incorporate were a baseball minigame and a magic cauldron that made hidden actors appear in the environment.


The primary software used for development was Unreal Engine. We also used Jira for project management and Github for version control. SketchFab, Mixamo, Quixel, and FreeSound.org were our primary sources for visual and auditory media.

Project 2: A2 Go!

Project 2 challenged us to create a parody on Pokemon Go!, one of the most famous AR mobile apps to date. In this version, you can walk around North Campus, visiting landmark locations and planting trees to make money, while fending off pesky squirrels. We also added a custom feature that allows users to change their skin after planting enough trees!


For this project, my partner and I used Unity for development. Jira was again used for project management, and Gilab provided us with version control. We sourced graphic and auditory elements from Mixamo, FreeSound.org, and the Unity Asset Store.

Project 3: drummvr

Our final project was an open-ended assignment, asking us to create a novel and socially-engaged XR product of our own. I worked in a team of four, and collectively we made drummvr: an interactive VR rhythm game designed as an alternative physical therapy method for individuals with muscular dystrophy. Our product features a variety of tracks, a fully customizable calibration mode to allow for all range of motions, and a complete marketing website to demonstrate key features. See the website for more details!
